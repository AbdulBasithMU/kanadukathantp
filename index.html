History of Town Panchayat

As per GO.No. 1603 L&M dated 13.06.1924 it was given the status of Grade First Town Panchayat in 1924. Its Assistant Director Office and Director Office are at Sivaganga Collectorate Complex and Kuralagam, Chennai respectively.

Kanadukathan Town Panchayat office was originally at the eastern bank of Korattaiar Tank of Pazhayoor revenue village. Then it was shifted at the present location is at the western side of the bus stop (IOB Entrance). Then under Infrastructure Development Programme in Backward Town Scheme, the foundation stone for the Town Panchayat Office complex was paid by the State Minister for Home, Govt of India, Hon P. Chidambaram on 02.07.1998.

About 400 years age, this area was a thick forest of Pala and Veera trees and Sankara plants enjoying a good amount of rains during monsoon and the people were engaged in cultivation in same area and parging taxes to the Sivaganga zamin.

Since they were protecting quarter portion area of the land, this had been named as Kanadukathan. The streets are uniform straight and clean as in france. At the edgn of the streets, water drains area made. In houses are palatial splendourous and very large in 6 blocks stretching to the full streets and can withstand spashing rains, floods and such natural calamitices.

It is a Pilgrim center in ancient epic and tourist attraction. The town panchayat is coming under municipal act from the year 1996. It is graded as Special Village Panchayat form 11.06.2004 wide G.O.No: 270, dated 04.06.2004. Again it has been upgraded as Town Panchayat much G.O.No. 55, dated 14.07.2006.

LOCATION:

Kanadukathan is one of the Town Panchayat located in the Sivagangai district. Tamil Nadu, It is Situated on NH210 Rameshwaram - Trichy Road at a distance of 13 from karaikudi Town and 60km from Sivagangai and 80km North side of Madurai. The Total area of the Selection Grade Town panchayat is 8.00Sq.km and Total Number of Streets is 59.

TOURIST ATTRACTION PLACES:

The chettinadu palace, Island Bungalow, Sivan Kovil, Big Temple and other places such as pillayarpatti, Thirukostiyur, Pattamagalam Guru Bhagavan kovil are the main tourist attractions in this district.

The film industry and the media are now concentrating in making the shooting in various palaces situated in and around Chettinadu. Therefore, there is a likely hood of improvement of tourists/ visitors in the future.

When enquired the caretaker of the palaces, it is understood that at present about 60000 to 70000 foreigners and 1,50,000 Indian tourists are visiting the Heritage town to see the palaces.

The Town has Two NATIONALISED BANK, ELECTRICITY BOARD SUB STATION WITH Office, Town panchayat office, a post and telegraph office, a police Station, and railway station, one library, there is a Govt. Hospital, One Elementary School, One Middle School, One Higher Secondary schools. There is one daily market and One Weekly market held on Sunday.
